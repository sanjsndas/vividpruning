What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or other devices) by websites you visit. They help the website remember your actions and preferences over time, enabling a more personalized and efficient browsing experience.

Cookies can be “session cookies” that expire once you close your browser, or “persistent cookies” that remain on your device for a specified period or until you delete them.

Types of Cookies We Use

We use several types of cookies on our website, each serving a different purpose:

Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. Without these, basic features like page navigation, security, and form submissions would not work effectively.

Performance and Analytics Cookies

These cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, such as pages visited, time spent, and any errors encountered. The data helps us understand website performance and improve user experience. The data is aggregated and anonymized.

Functional Cookies

Functional cookies remember choices you make such as language preferences, login details, or region, enhancing your browsing experience by personalizing the website content.

Advertising and Tracking Cookies

We use these cookies to deliver advertisements relevant to your interests, measure the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns, and limit the number of times you see an ad. These may be set by us or by third-party advertising partners.

How We Use Cookies

Most browsers provide tools to manage the cookies stored on your device. You can:

A: Yes, you can disable cookies in your browser settings, but this may affect the usability of certain websites.

Q: Are cookies safe?

Glossary of Common Cookie Terms

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser.
  • Persistent Cookies: Cookies that remain on your device until deleted or expired.
  • First-Party Cookies: Set by the website you are visiting.
  • Third-Party Cookies: Set by domains other than the one you are visiting, often for advertising or analytics.
  • Secure Cookies: Cookies that are only transmitted over secure HTTPS connections.
  • HttpOnly Cookies: Cookies inaccessible to client-side scripts, providing protection against cross-site scripting attacks.
